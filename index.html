Popular Mountains in Western Washington

Mount Rainier

Mount Rainier, also refered to as Tahoma, is a large active stratovolcano in
the Cascade mountain range in the Pacific Northwest. Not only is this the
tallest mountain in Washington State, it is also the tallest mountain in the
entire Cascade mountain range.


Mount Rainier Information


Mount St. Helens

Mount St. Helens, is an active stratovolcano located in Skamania County,
Washington. While it used to stand tall as the 5th tallest mountain in
Washington state, that changed during 1980 when the mountain had an extreamly
violent eruption, which remains the deadliest and most economically destructive
volcanic event in recorded U.S. history.

Mount Baker

Mount Baker, is an active glacier covered andesitic stratovolcano in the Cascade
mountain range. Mount Baker has the second-most thermally active crater in the
Cascade Range after Mount St. Helens. After Mount Rainier, Mount Baker has the
heaviest glacier cover of the Cascade Range volcanoes;

Mount Adams

Mount Adams, known by some Native American tribes as Pahto or Klickitat,
is a potentially active stratovolcano in the Cascade Range. Although Adams
has not erupted in more than 1,000 years, it is not considered extinct. It is
the second-highest mountain in Washington, after Mount Rainier.
